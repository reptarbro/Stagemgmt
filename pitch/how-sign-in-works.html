The short version
How signing in works, and why there is no password

Standby uses modern passwordless sign-in. You never create or remember a password. Instead, you prove it is you by receiving a one-time link at your own email. Here is exactly what happens, why it is secure, and how your shows stay organized under one account.

Local first
Works with no account
Create a production and run your whole show on one device without ever signing in. Signing in is optional, and only adds syncing.
🔑 Passwordless
Nothing to forget
No password to set, store, or leak. You sign in with a single-use link sent to your email, the same method banks and Slack use.
🔒 Private by design
Only you can read it
Your cast contacts and emergency details are locked to your account. No one else, and no other user, can reach them.

Signing in, step by step

1

Type your email

On the Settings screen, open Cloud Sync and enter your email address. That address is the only thing you provide.

2

We send a link

Standby asks its secure server to email you a one-time sign-in link. The app already knows how to reach that server, so nothing is set up in advance.

3

Open it on that device

Tap the link in your inbox on the same device. Your account is created automatically the first time, with no separate signup.

4

You are in

You return to Standby signed in. Push your show to the cloud, then Pull it onto any other device where you sign in with the same email.

Is it safe without a password?

Yes, and it is generally safer. A password can be guessed, reused across sites, phished, or leaked in a breach. A one-time link cannot: it works once, it expires quickly, and it only helps someone who can already open your email inbox. Access to your inbox becomes the proof of identity, which is exactly how password resets already work on every other service, just without the extra password step in front.

Remember this

Your email is your account. There is no registration form and no password vault. The first time you request a link, the account is created for that address on the spot. Sign in with the same email anywhere, and the same shows follow you.

Frequently asked questions

QDo I even need to sign in?
No. Standby is fully usable on one device with no account. Sign in only when you want the same show on your iPad, phone, and computer at once.
QHow does the app email me if I never registered?
The app ships already knowing how to reach Standby's secure server. When you type your email, it asks that server to send the link, and your account is created for that address on the spot.
QWhat if the email does not arrive?
Check spam or promotions, then use Resend. Free email has a small hourly limit, so if you are signing in on several devices at once, wait a few minutes between them.
QIs a one-time link really secure?
Yes. The link works once, expires quickly, and only helps someone who can already open your inbox. It cannot be reused, and there is no stored password to steal.
QWhat if I lose access to my email?
The cloud copy is tied to that address, so keep a file backup as insurance (Settings, Export). A second sign-in method with Google is on the roadmap for extra safety.
QCan anyone else see my cast's contacts?
No. The server enforces that only your signed-in account can read your data. The key inside the app cannot bypass that rule, so no other user can reach your shows.
QDo I need internet to use Standby?
No. Everything works offline on your device. Internet is only needed at the moment you Push or Pull to sync.
QHow do I use it on more than one device?
Sign in with the same email on each device. Push from the device that has your latest work, then Pull on the others.
QCan I keep more than one production?
Yes. All of your shows live under one account. Switch between them from the sidebar, with no extra login per show.
QCan I archive an old show?
Soon. Archiving will move a finished production into a separate list, hidden from your day to day view but never deleted, and reopenable anytime.
QCan my ASM or team share a show with me?
Not yet. Today each person has their own account. Shared productions for a team are planned. For now you can hand off a full backup file to a colleague.
QWhat does an account cost?
Nothing. Accounts and cloud sync are free.
QCan I sign in with Google instead?
That option is planned. Google sign-in will let you sign in with one tap and no email step at all, alongside the email link.
QHow do I sign out or switch accounts?
Open Settings, then Cloud Sync, and tap Sign out. Your data stays on the device, and you can sign back in, or use a different email, anytime.
QIs this less legitimate than a password app?
No. One-time links and codes are used by banks, Slack, and government portals. It is a recognized, secure standard, not a shortcut.
QWill I get signed out at random?
No. Once a device is signed in, it stays signed in until you choose to sign out, so you are not asked for a new link every time you open the app.
The bottom line

Passwordless sign-in is not a workaround, it is a deliberate, modern choice used across banking, healthcare, and major workplace tools. It removes the weakest link in most accounts, the password, while keeping your data private to you and portable across every device you use.
